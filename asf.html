OUR GOAL WAS TO INFORM

The outbreak of the disease in the provinces of Southern Highlands, Enga and Hela meant that our goal was to inform villagers, farmers and the general public about the signs and symptoms of ASF. We developed a creative campaign to equip them with practical prevention methods and warn against false or harmful messages.

Our campaign delivered a list of dos and don’ts

We used emotive photography accompanied with striking graphic icons to deliver concise information in both English and Tok Pisin across a range of channels including press placements, social media posts in addition to animations on TV and radio. Our campaign delivered a list of dos and don’ts that could be easily understood and implemented, whilst providing a number to call to report sick or dead pigs.

Swine Fever has been contained

Since its outbreak, the spread of African Swine Fever has been contained to three initial provinces, helping to limit economic losses, support local businesses and bolster community confidence. TRIBE is proud of our role in assisting the national biosecurity agency, NAQIA, to protect PNG pigs and people’s way of life.
